In the phase space representation the wave action density is a field over the physical coordinates, \(\mathbf{x}\), and the wave numbers \(\mathbf{k}\). To efficiently solve the wave action dynamics we represent the wave action density in terms of Lagrangian ray volumes (c.f. Muraschko et al. 2015). One important property of the wave is the conservation of phase space volume as follows \[ \begin{align} 0 &= \nabla_{\mathbf{X}}\cdot\mathbf{c}_g + \nabla_\mathbf{k}\cdot\dot{\mathbf{k}}. \end{align} \] We then approximate the phase space wave action density as small volumes each formed around a carrier ray which evolves according to the modulation equations.

Due to the approximation as discrete rectangular volumes ray volumes may overlap or drift apart during the modulated evolution. Their effect on the mean flow may then be integrated in wave number space and projected as described below.
