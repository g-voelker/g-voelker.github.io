In order to simulate the dynamics of internal gravity waves in the atmosphere we make use of the Bretherton-Grimshaw modulation equations. These can be found using a WKBJ multi-scale ansatz to find a special solution of the compressible Euler equations (e.g. Achatz et al. 2010; Achatz et al. 2017). One thus finds the evolution of a wave with a position, \(\mathbf{r}_w\), and a wave vector, \(\mathbf{k}\), to be described by the following set of equations:

\[ \begin{align} \omega(\mathbf{r}, t) &= \Omega(\mathbf{r}, \mathbf{k}, t) \\ &= \mathbf{k}\cdot\mathbf{u}(\mathbf{r}, t) + \sqrt{\frac{f^2(\mathbf{r})m^2 + N^2(\mathbf{r}, t)(k^2 + l^2)}{k^2 + l^2 + m^2}},\\ (\partial_t &+ \mathbf{c}_g\cdot\nabla_{\mathbf{r}})\mathbf{k} = \dot{\mathbf{k}} = -\nabla_{\mathbf{x}}\Omega, \\ (\partial_t &+ \mathbf{c}_g\cdot\nabla_{\mathbf{r}})\mathbf{r}_w = \dot{\mathbf{r}}_w = \nabla_{\mathbf{k}}\Omega. \end{align} \]

Where the group velocity is defined as \(\mathbf{c}_g = \nabla_{\mathbf{k}}\Omega\). Note that the gradient terms of the right hand comprise metric terms when using the spherical coordinate system as denoted by Hasha et al. (2008). The wave action equation including dissipation may then be written in phase space as follows (c.f. Muraschko et al. 2015):

\[ \begin{align} 0 &= \partial_t\mathcal{N} + \nabla_\mathbf{r}\cdot(\mathbf{c}_g\mathcal{N}) + \nabla_\mathbf{k}\cdot(\dot{\mathbf{k}}\mathcal{N}) + S \end{align} \] with the phase space wave action density \(\mathcal{N} = \mathcal{N}(\mathbf{r}, \mathbf{k}, t)\) and a dissipation term, \(S\). At the same time the mean flow is modulated through

\[ \begin{align} d_t\Theta &\propto {\color{dark-green}-\nabla_h\cdot\overline{\mathbf{u}'\theta'}}, \\ d_t\mathbf{U} &\propto - \frac{1}{\bar{\rho}}\nabla\cdot(\bar{\rho}\overline{\mathbf{u'}\mathbf{v'}}) + \frac{f}{g}\mathbf{e}_z\times\overline{\mathbf{u'}b'}, \end{align} \]

with the primed quantities being the wave perturbation fields (e.g. Wei et al. 2019). From the set of equations it is evident that the internal gravity waves can easily be described on the wave characteristics, that is along their path of propagation. At the same time the mean flow is typically simulated in a finite difference or finite volume sense on a Eulerian grid.
