We implement MS-GWaM into the high top model UA-ICON. All runs use the R2B4 grid specififcation (160km resolution) with 120 vertical layers for a model top at 150km. Bölöni et al. (2021) and Kim et al. (2021) showed that the columnar, i.e. one dimensional implementation of MS-GWaM produces reasonable structures in the simulated winds and temperatures. Here we drop the assumption of a strictly columnar propagation and instead introduce the fully three dimensional implementation of MS-GWaM. As before the wave model contains various non-orographic wave sources, non-dissipative wave-mean-flow interactions as well as wave breaking. An eﬃcient parallelization of the Lagrangian ray-tracing scheme allows for simulations with transient wave eﬀects in reasonable computation times.

Comparing simulation result composites for June for ICON with MS-GWaM in one and three dimensions with the URAP climatology one may find that the the parametrization generally performs as expected with comparable zonal-mean zonal winds (Fig. 9). Note that the three dimensional setup has not been tuned to ensure comparability with the one dimensional implementation.

Comparing the global horizontal average of the tendency of the phase space integrated wave action field from shorter simulations we find that the three dimensional propagation does not significantly change the global wave energy balance (Fig. 10). THe horizontal distribution of wave action, momentum fluxes and winds does, however, differ significantly.
